Land Use Classification Models

This is an example of a land use map I created of my hometown, Karachi, Pakistan. Being an increasingly urbanizing city, Karachi has had depleting amounts of vegetation present within its urban centers. Within this map, this is increasingly prominent. Through a false-colour composite created using spectral data, we can see the vegetation present within the city highlighted in bright red. Meanwhile, urban areas are simply white.

This lack of vegetation became further prominent through the use of a supervised learning algorithm, which classified the presence of vegetation in bright green and urban areas in a light beige. Similarly, we can see this through the NDVI spectral data analysis of Karachi, seen below. Notice how the spectral identity for most of the city is relatively the same due to the high presence of construction material present and a lack of vegetation other than sparse mangroves. Through the NDVI analysis below, I primarily highlighted urban land use classifications to indicate this prominence. Maps, such as these, serve as ideal tools for mapping sustainable cities since they illustrate a problem clearly and the areas needed to address for a more sustainable future.
